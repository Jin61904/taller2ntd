Quantum information processing in molecular magnets

Camila Galindo - 506222700
Computación Cuántica Imanes Moleculares

Ficha Técnica


Título: Quantum information processing in molecular magnets
Autores: D. Stepanenko, N.E. Bonesteel, D.P. DiVincenzo, G. Burkard, D. Loss
Resumen: The motivation behind this effort comes both from the technological appeal of new and better computers and from the fundamental scientific questions about the nature of information and the physical limits on our ability to process it. Quantum mechanics enters this discussion with understanding that an abstract information is always embedded in some real world objects and therefore it is governed by the laws of nature which are quantum. From the point of view of technology, the appeal of quantum computers comes from the fact that there are problems that are tractable on quantum computers which are believed to be intractable on classical ones. The most famous such problem is factoring of integers into their prime factors, suggesting that quantum computers may be intrinsically more powerful than classical ones.
Año de publicación: 2008
Referencia completa: D. Stepanenko et al., Inorganica Chimica Acta 361 (2008) 3740–3745.
Lugar de publicación:Revista Inorganica Chimica Acta
